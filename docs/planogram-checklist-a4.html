☕ RLC Café — Planogram Setup

5-minute Sunday checklist · keep beside the fridge

🧊 Fridge

Top shelf — Milk zone

  • Open Milk carton at front-left, sealed cartons behind
  • Open Oat carton at front-left of its row, sealed behind
  • Red divider tape visible between Milk row and Oat row
  • All cartons label forward

Middle shelf — Opened condiments

  • Lemon Juice (Liotti), Nata de Coco, Citron Paste — each in its own cell
  • Lids match real state (off if scoop is in use)
  • Liquid level easy to see through the bottle/jar

Bottom shelf — Chilled fizz

  • Sprite cluster on the left (label forward)
  • Sparkling Water cluster in the middle
  • Tonic on the right edge (visual anchor)
  • No interleaving brands

Door / Freezer

  • Ice in bagged form (not loose tray) — count bags
  • Whipped cream cans, label forward
  • Door pockets clear of personal items

🗄 Storeroom

Shelf 1 — Coffee

  • REGULAR cluster on the left, DECAF on the right
  • ~30 cm gap between them
  • Big handwritten REGULAR / DECAF signs visible
  • Open bag clipped upright, sealed bags behind

Shelf 2 — Powders

  • Fixed order: Tiger 63 → Unicorn 50 → Matcha → Chai
  • Top label visible on every tub (write with marker if missing)
  • Open container in front, sealed reserves behind
  • Houjicha removed if stock is 0

Shelf 3 — Monin syrups

  • Order: Passion → Lemon → Orange → Butterfly Pea → Sugar
  • All labels facing forward — non-negotiable
  • Pump on the open bottle, sealed bottle behind

Shelf 4 — Teas, jars

  • English Breakfast box open, sachets visible
  • Spare boxes on the right edge

Shelf 5 / floor — Bulk reserves

  • Sprite, Sparkling, Tonic cases — one row deep
  • If second row needed, plan a second photo at angle
  • Sealed Nata de Coco jars only (open jars live in the fridge)

📏 Six rules that matter most

  1. Labels forward, always. Especially Monin syrups, milks, and powders.
  2. Open in front, sealed behind. Same item, two states — the AI counts each differently.
  3. One row, one item type. Don't mix Sprite and Sparkling.
  4. Same place, every Sunday. The reference photo expects it.
  5. Three photos max. Fridge usually 1, storeroom often 2, never more than 3.
  6. Edit if the AI is wrong. Trust your eyes over the model when they disagree.

📷 Taking the photo

Distance
~1 metre back. Every label readable at 100 % zoom on the phone.
Orientation
Phone landscape (wider field, less skew).
Angle
Phone parallel to the shelf face — don't tilt up or down.
Lighting
Door fully open. Use room lights, not the fridge bulb. Avoid backlight from windows.
Framing
Centre on the middle shelf. Show a little empty space around items so the AI gets spatial reference.
Splitting
Tall storeroom rack: shelf 1+2 in one frame, 3+4 in another, shelf 5 separately if needed.

🔴 If the AI keeps getting it wrong

  • Confidence stays red on a row for 2+ weeks → fix layout first
  • Re-take reference photo only if a real item changed — not for one-off disruptions
  • Items not on the inventory list will show as "unknown" — keep them out of frame
  • Edit the count manually before tapping Confirm
